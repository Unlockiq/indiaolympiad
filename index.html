Straight answers

Olympiads in India — briefly explained.

What is a school olympiad, and is it official?

School olympiads are competitive exams run by private organisations, trusts and academic bodies for Classes 1–12. They are enrichment competitions — not conducted by CBSE, NCERT or any government body, and participation is entirely optional. Their value lies in building exam temperament, competitive benchmarking and structured practice at a level beyond the textbook.

How many olympiads should a child attempt per year?

For most children, one or two well-chosen olympiads beat five scattered ones. A common pattern: one subject olympiad in the child's strongest area plus one reasoning or diagnostic exam. That's enough exposure without crowding the school calendar or creating exam anxiety.

What do olympiads cost?

Registration fees for most school olympiads in India range from roughly ₹150 to ₹500 per exam. Fees are listed on each olympiad's entry in our directory so you can compare before registering — check the organiser's official site for the final fee before payment.

Are online olympiads credible?

Several well-established olympiads now run entirely online with proctoring — webcam monitoring and timed windows that maintain fairness. Credibility depends on the organiser's proctoring standards and result transparency, not on the mode itself. We note the format per listing.

How do olympiads relate to NEP 2020 and competency-based learning?

India's National Education Policy 2020 emphasises competency and application over rote recall. Olympiad-style questions — multi-step, applied, reasoning-heavy — sit naturally in that direction. Individual olympiads remain independent programmes by their respective organisers, not government initiatives, but the pedagogical alignment is real.

How is this directory different from others?

Most olympiad directories are prep-selling funnels that rank olympiads they have a commercial stake in. This directory is published by an organiser who also runs olympiads listed here — and applies the same neutral criteria to all listings. That conflict of interest is disclosed in writing in our footer. No listing pays for placement.
